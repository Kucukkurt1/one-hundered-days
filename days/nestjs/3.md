## üìñ Task Explanations

Providers, and more specifically Services, are a core concept in NestJS. They are responsible for encapsulating business logic, keeping your controllers lean and focused on handling HTTP requests. Today, you will learn how to create a service and use NestJS's powerful Dependency Injection (DI) system to provide it to your controller. This separation of concerns is crucial for writing testable and maintainable code.

## üí° What You'll Gain

By the end of today, you will understand how to properly structure your application's logic by separating it into services. You will also have a foundational understanding of how Dependency Injection works in NestJS, which is a key pattern for building robust applications.

## üìö Dictionary for Developers

*   **`@Injectable()`:** A decorator that marks a class as a provider that can be managed by the NestJS IoC (Inversion of Control) container.
*   **Dependency Injection (DI):** A design pattern in which a class receives its dependencies from an external source rather than creating them itself.
*   **Service:** A type of provider that is typically used to encapsulate business logic.
